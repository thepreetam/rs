## I. Executive Synthesis

Rimini Street stands at a genuine inflection point. The Q1 2026 earnings release revealed an underlying business whose core growth (5.2% ex-PeopleSoft) and forward-looking indicators—Remaining Performance Obligations at $643.6M (+16.4% YoY), Adjusted Billings at +22.9%—tell a far more compelling story than the headline 1.2% revenue growth suggests. The Agentic AI thesis is real, the ServiceNow partnership is strategically consequential, and the Oracle PeopleSoft settlement has removed a decade-long litigation overhang.

The fundamental tension in this analysis is between velocity and magnitude. The strategic bull case sees a high probability of reaching $15/share by 2028, emphasizing the massive valuation disconnect (~0.7x P/S vs. 4-6x for software peers) and the structural inevitability of a re-rating as recognized revenue catches up to the billings curve. The forensic stress-test identifies critical overstatements in near-term revenue targets, a mathematical error in Rule of 40 timing, and competitive threats that are underweighted in the bull case.

This document does not arbitrate between these positions—it integrates them. Rimini Street is both undervalued and execution-risky: a "Disruptive Value" story whose upside is validated by structural market forces but whose timeline is likely compressed by internal and competitive frictions.

### 2.2 The Leading Indicator Divergence

The surge in Adjusted Calculated Billings (+22.9%) and RPO (+16.4%) against recognized revenue growth of +1.2% creates a divergence that is typical for a company transitioning revenue mix toward new product lines. Under ASC 606, subscription revenue from new AI solutions lags contract signing by one to three quarters depending on implementation complexity. The billings-to-revenue conversion gap means approximately $90-110M of unrecognized revenue sits in the backlog, scheduled for recognition over the next 12-18 months.

This divergence has two interpretations. The bullish view: recognized revenue will mechanically accelerate as the backlog converts, creating a visible and predictable re-rating catalyst within 2-4 quarters. The skeptical view: RPO includes support contracts with multi-year durations where revenue is recognized evenly over the contract life, meaning the conversion is linear rather than lumpy, and the revenue acceleration will be gradual rather than step-function.

The truth likely lies between these extremes. Some portion of the RPO is multi-year support contracts (smooth recognition), while the Agentic AI component carries shorter implementation cycles and will convert faster. Without a formal "Revenue Recognition Bridge" from management providing the RPO composition breakdown (support duration by cohort, AI implementation timelines), the market cannot distinguish between these two components—which is precisely why management should provide this disclosure.

### 2.3 The Investment Trough

The collapse in Adjusted EBITDA margins from 15.7% to 8.4% and the EPS miss of 50% versus consensus represent the most significant near-term friction. Management attributed this compression to "additional strategic investments in new AI and innovation offerings" and "streamlining global operations." R&D expenses reached $0.57M as a separately reported line item for the first time.

The full-year 2026 EBITDA margin guidance of 12.5-15.5% implies significant operating leverage in H2 2026. To achieve even the midpoint (14%), quarterly EBITDA margins must average approximately 17-18% in H2, roughly double the Q1 run rate. This requires either: (a) the AI build phase completing as planned, with marginal costs declining as deployment scales, (b) the support delivery model achieving automation-driven labor efficiencies, or (c) revenue acceleration pulling through fixed-cost leverage.

All three mechanisms are plausible. None is assured. The Q2 2026 earnings report is the first data point confirming whether the margin recovery trajectory is on track.

The strongest validated case study is Ypê, a Brazilian CPG company with 22,000+ business customers. Ypê deployed Rimini Agentic UX for EDI order exception handling on top of SAP. Published and attributable results include:
- 80% reduction in exception handling time
- 60% reduction in approval cycle time
- 100% audit readiness
- Deployment in under one month

Ypê CIO Geraldo Pereira's attributable testimonial—"Before Rimini Agentic UX, we had eight steps across customer service, sales and other departments. Now, it's simply two"—represents the single strongest independent validation of the Agentic AI value proposition.

### 3.2 The Remaining Case Study Base

Every other published client reference requires more careful classification:

**Lotte Rental** (South Korea): Extended from a 1-year Oracle support contract to a 3-year Oracle + 3-year SAP support contract, achieving 50%+ annual support fee savings with projected 100,000 work hours saved over five years. This is a support expansion win with a stated intention to reinvest savings into AI, RPA, and cloud capabilities—not an Agentic AI deployment. The ROI metrics are real but accrue to the support business.

**Melitta Group** (Germany): Pilot deployment for Material SKU Creation in SAP. Melitta Head of IT Daniel Buono confirmed "clear potential to streamline a historically manual process." This is explicitly a pilot—not a production deployment—with no disclosed quantified ROI metrics.

**Synthesized assessment**: The strongest validated proof point is Ypê—a production deployment with independently attributable, quantified results delivered within four months of the Agentic UX platform launch. This is exactly what early-stage enterprise AI traction looks like: a flagship reference client, 26 pilots in the pipeline, and the remainder of the 3,130-client installed base as a cross-sell opportunity. The more relevant metric is the pilot-to-production conversion rate over the next four quarters, not the static ratio against the full support client base. If Rimini converts even 10-15% of the 26 pilots into published, quantified case studies by Q1 2027, that trajectory would be consistent with enterprise AI platform adoption patterns at comparable scale.

## IV. Market Opportunity and Competitive Landscape

### 4.1 The Agentic AI ERP Market (2026-2030)

The market for Agentic AI applied to enterprise resource planning is moving from the Peak of Inflated Expectations into the Slope of Enlightenment. Gartner forecasts enterprise spend on supply chain management software with agentic AI capabilities will grow from under $2B in 2025 to approximately $53B by 2030. IDC projects 2.2 billion active AI agents globally by 2030, executing tasks at a 524% CAGR. BCG estimates $200B in net new demand for AI integration services by 2030.

The specific opportunity Rimini targets is the intersection of two markets:
- The third-party ERP support market: $10.5B in 2024, growing at 8.9% CAGR to $22.3B by 2033
- The enterprise agentic AI market: $2.6B in 2024, growing at ~45% CAGR to $24.5B by 2030

Rimini's addressable installed base is substantial. Of approximately 35,000 SAP ECC customers, only 39% have migrated to S/4HANA after nearly a decade of vendor pressure. The remaining 61%—roughly 21,000 customers—continue running ECC, creating a structural tailwind for Rimini's "no disruption" value proposition. SAP's January 2026 guidance cut and resulting 22% stock decline validated that the installed base is actively resisting vendor-mandated migration timelines.

### 4.2 Competitive Positioning

Rimini faces competition on four fronts, each with different threat profiles:

**SAP Joule**: The most direct competitive threat. Joule has 2,400+ skills embedded across S/4HANA, SuccessFactors, Ariba, and Analytics Cloud as of Q1 2026. Joule Studio (Agent Builder) is generally available. SAP's multi-agent orchestration, Agent-to-Agent (A2A) interoperability protocol, and Model Context Protocol (MCP) server support position Joule as a comprehensive enterprise agent platform. Critically, Joule is bundled at no incremental cost for S/4HANA Cloud customers. Rimini's counterargument—that Joule requires S/4HANA migration (a multi-million dollar, multi-year project with high failure risk)—is valid but narrows the competitive aperture.

**Oracle AI**: Oracle has embedded AI agents directly into Fusion Cloud workflows as process-native agents rather than a separate interface, with AI included at no extra cost. For Oracle's on-premise installed base (PeopleSoft, EBS, JD Edwards), third-party support remains the only path to cost savings, but the Oracle settlement requires Rimini to wind down PeopleSoft support by July 2028. Oracle's process-native approach reduces Rimini's "over-the-top" advantage because the AI lives inside the system rather than above it.

**ServiceNow**: Simultaneously Rimini's most important partner and its most significant long-term competitive threat. ServiceNow's AI Agent Orchestrator, AI Agent Studio, and AI Control Tower position it as the definitive enterprise workflow orchestration layer. With $13.96B TTM revenue growing at 22%, ServiceNow has the scale to eventually build competing ERP-native agent capabilities. The partnership gives ServiceNow ERP domain access without investment; if the market proves large enough, ServiceNow has three options: deepen the partnership (favorable to Rimini), build internally (existential threat), or acquire Rimini (outright win for shareholders).

**UiPath and Pegasystems**: Adjacent rather than direct competitors in the automation and orchestration space. Neither directly targets legacy ERP modernization as a primary use case, but both could extend into this space. Pega CEO Alan Trefler's sharp criticism of multi-agent approaches as "delusional" indirectly supports Rimini's more pragmatic, process-specific agent model—but also highlights the vendor skepticism that Rimini must overcome in enterprise sales cycles.

### 4.3 The Composable Moat Assessment

Rimini's competitive moat rests on five pillars with varying defensibility:

**Support Market Dominance (86%+ share)**: Deepest, most defensible moat. 3,130 active clients including 78 Fortune 500 companies, scale advantages in engineering talent, knowledge base depth, and geographic coverage (54% international revenue). However, this moat is naturally eroding as the PeopleSoft wind-down removes a revenue stream by 2028.

**Over-the-Top Deployment Model**: Most differentiated value proposition. Deployment in "days and weeks" versus multi-year ERP migration projects. The Ypê case study validates this claim. However, this advantage is replicable—ServiceNow, UiPath, or Pega could theoretically build similar layers. Rimini's 20 years of ERP domain expertise provides a head start, not a permanent barrier.

**Patents and Proprietary IP**: Moderate at best. Three proprietary AI applications for support delivery (Case Assignment Advisor, C-Signal, Case Assistant) reduced resolution times by 23%. The Agentic UX platform is built on ServiceNow, meaning much of the underlying technology is licensed.

**Client Base and Retention**: Genuine commercial moat. 88% retention, 4.9/5.0 satisfaction, Executive Sponsor Program creating deep C-level relationships. However, Agentic AI is a new, unproven cross-sell—clients trust Rimini for support, but will they trust Rimini for AI transformation?

**Self-Funding Economics**: Most financially elegant insight. By saving clients 50-90% on support fees, Rimini creates a "found money" pool that funds Agentic AI pilots without new budget approvals. No competitor can match the combination of support savings plus AI innovation in a single vendor relationship.

## V. Agentic AI Revenue Ramp: The Critical Assumption

### 5.1 The 15% Target Under Stress

The target of 15%+ Agentic AI revenue contribution by Q4 2026 is the single most aggressive near-term projection in any analysis of Rimini's trajectory. A rigorous stress-test reveals the magnitude of the gap:

To achieve 15% of total revenue (~$66M+ at 6% growth) from a product that had negligible P&L impact as of Q4 2025, Rimini would need approximately $65-68M in Agentic AI revenue in FY2026. At an assumed average deal size of $200,000 (support plus AI solutions), this requires 325+ Agentic AI deals in 8 months (May-December 2026). With approximately 20 new enterprise sellers hired in late 2025/early 2026, each seller would need to close 16+ Agentic deals in their first year—a ramp that exceeds established productivity benchmarks for enterprise software sales.

CEO Seth Ravin's own characterization of Agentic AI adoption as "early stages" with the "hockey stick" expected in the back half of 2026 "at earliest" is inconsistent with the 15% target. The CEO is signaling caution; the target implies acceleration without precedent.

**Realistic range**: 5-8% Agentic AI revenue mix by Q4 2026. This is a 50-67% reduction from plan but does not invalidate the long-term thesis. The hockey stick is real but arrives in 2027, not 2026.

### 5.2 Timeline Realignment

The hockey stick inflection point depends on three structural factors:

1. **Enterprise procurement cycles**: AI procurement cycles average 6-9 months for new category purchases. First Agentic UX deals (January 2026) cycle into recognized revenue no earlier than Q3 2026-Q1 2027.

2. **Sales force productivity ramp**: New enterprise sellers (20-25% headcount expansion) require 3-4 quarters for full quota capacity. Sellers hired in Q1-Q2 2026 reach productivity in Q1-Q2 2027.

3. **Referenceable case study base**: Sales cycle compression requires 5-7 published case studies with quantified ROI. At current publication velocity, this does not reach critical mass before Q1 2027.

The synthesized timeline: Phase 1 (2026) proves product-market fit with 3-5 referenceable deployments. Phase 2 (2027) accelerates with 100-200 clients. Phase 3 (2028+) scales to 500+ clients.

## VI. The ServiceNow Partnership: Deep Analysis

### 6.1 Current State

The partnership, as publicly disclosed, involves 26 joint pilots underway as of December 2025, joint product development (Agentic UX runs on the ServiceNow Now Platform), and mutual client references. There is no public evidence of a formal co-sell quota, joint sales compensation, or dedicated ServiceNow salesforce carrying Rimini Agentic UX.

The partnership is currently a technology integration with go-to-market aspirations. This is not unusual for enterprise platform relationships of this maturity (approximately 12-18 months since public announcement), but it means the distribution channel acceleration that the bull case assumes has not yet materialized.

### 6.2 Three Strategic Outcomes

ServiceNow's long-term strategic posture toward Rimini can take three forms:

**Option 1: Deepened Partnership (Favorable)** . ServiceNow certifies Rimini Agentic UX as a strategic ISV solution, establishes co-sell targets, provides dedicated sales resources, and potentially takes an equity stake. This would validate the bull case distribution thesis and significantly reduce customer acquisition costs.

**Option 2: Build Internally (Existential Threat)** . If ServiceNow determines that ERP-aware agent capabilities are core to its platform strategy, it could invest in competing capabilities—either organically through its AI Agent Studio roadmap or through acquisition of a different ERP specialist. ServiceNow's $200B+ market cap gives it the resources to do this without financial constraint.

**Option 3: Acquire Rimini (Outright Win)** . ServiceNow acquires Rimini Street at a substantial premium to current market value, gaining instant ERP domain expertise, 3,130 client relationships, and a proven over-the-top deployment model.

The synthesized assessment assigns probabilities: Option 1 (50-55%), Option 2 (25-30%), Option 3 (15-20%). The "Status Quo" outcome from Knowledge 2026 — formal co-sell framework without exclusivity — is the most probable near-term result, maintaining optionality without immediate acceleration.

### 6.3 The Knowledge 2026 Inflection

ServiceNow Knowledge 2026 (May 5-7) is the critical near-term catalyst for the partnership's evolution. The synthesized framework for evaluating the outcome:

**Elevated**: ServiceNow announces Rimini as a certified strategic ISV with co-sell go-to-market, co-branded sales collateral, and dedicated pipeline targets. Immediate re-rating catalyst.

**Status Quo**: ServiceNow features Rimini as a partner case study, demonstrates joint deployments, but stops short of formal co-sell. Partnership optionality preserved without acceleration.

**Marginalized**: ServiceNow highlights AI Agent Orchestrator/AI Control Tower as independent ERP capabilities, positioning Rimini as one of many ISV partners. Accelerates existential threat timeline.

Status Quo is assessed as the most probable outcome (55%), given that ServiceNow has not historically elevated individual ISV partners to co-sell status without extended validation.

## VII. PeopleSoft Wind-Down: The Known Unknown

### 7.1 Magnitude and Timeline

The Oracle PeopleSoft wind-down is the most immediately quantifiable risk. PeopleSoft support generated approximately $30M in annual revenue. The July 7, 2025 settlement agreement mandates full wind-down by July 31, 2028. The wind-down creates a persistent 2-3 percentage point drag on headline revenue growth through the transition period.

In Q1 2026, U.S. revenue declined 6.4% primarily due to this wind-down. International growth of 8.3% partially offset the decline but created a "treading water" effect on total reported growth. The execution plan must accelerate non-PeopleSoft revenue by $10-15M per year just to maintain flat total growth.

### 7.2 Replacement Strategy Assessment

The PeopleSoft revenue replacement challenge is structurally difficult for three reasons:
1. PeopleSoft support margins are among the highest in Rimini's portfolio, meaning replacement dollar-for-dollar is insufficient—replacement revenue must carry higher margins to maintain overall margin trajectory.
2. The wind-down timeline is fixed and accelerating, not flexible.
3. Oracle will compete aggressively to recapture PeopleSoft clients during the transition.

The most realistic replacement path involves three parallel strategies:
- Migrate PeopleSoft clients to Oracle EBS or JD Edwards support (the settlement restricts PeopleSoft specifically, not all Oracle support, and EBS/JD Edwards are not covered by the wind-down)
- Convert PeopleSoft support clients to Agentic UX clients on whatever ERP they migrate to
- Accelerate new logo acquisition in international markets where PeopleSoft exposure is minimal

The probability of fully offsetting the $30M revenue gap by mid-2027 is assessed at 40-50%—achievable but requiring discipline.

## IX. The Rule of 40 Gap

### 9.1 The Timing Problem

No realistic scenario achieves Rule of 40 (combined revenue growth rate + EBITDA margin >= 40%) before 2028, and even the Flawless scenario reaches exactly 40.0%—not "40+"—in 2028. McKinsey research confirms that only one-third of SaaS companies achieve Rule of 40 and only 16% sustain it.

| Year | Conservative | Base | Flawless |
|------|-------------|------|----------|
| 2026 (growth + EBITDA) | 5% + 13.5% = 18.5% | 6% + 14.5% = 20.5% | 7.5% + 15.5% = 23.0% |
| 2027 | 8% + 15.5% = 23.5% | 12% + 17% = 29.0% | 15% + 18.5% = 33.5% |
| 2028 | 10% + 17.5% = 27.5% | 15% + 20% = 35.0% | 18% + 22% = 40.0% |
| 2029 (projected) | 10% + 19% = 29% | 15% + 22% = 37% | 18% + 24% = 42% |

### 9.2 Implications for the Re-Rating

The Rule of 40 gap means the P/E multiple expansion from 8-9x to 20-25x depends on a narrative shift—investors accepting the "AI platform" story before the empirical metric of Rule of 40 is achieved. This is possible but requires consistent beat-and-raise quarters, referenceable case study production, and analyst coverage initiation. The alternative path—waiting for Rule of 40 achievement before the re-rating—pushes the $15 target to 2029-2030.

### 10.2 The Gartner AI Failure Rate

Gartner's forecast that over 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027 is arguably the most underappreciated systematic risk to the Rimini story. The projected failure modes—escalating costs, unclear ROI, inadequate risk controls—are directly relevant to Rimini's client base. The shift to Agentic AI introduces a new cost structure: AI inference tokens. As agents move from isolated pilots to parallel execution, compute costs can escalate exponentially. Rimini's gross margin dropped to 59.0% in Q1 2026, partly from AI infrastructure costs. If the company does not establish "FinOps for Agentic AI" as a core discipline, increased inference costs could permanently cap gross margins in the high 50s, preventing the 18-22% EBITDA margin target.

## XII. M&A Optionality

### 12.1 Acquisition Framework

Rimini's current market valuation embeds a meaningful discount to any reasonable takeout valuation. At approximately $3.35/share (market cap $313M), enterprise value adjusting for $132.2M cash and $58.4M debt is approximately $240M.
